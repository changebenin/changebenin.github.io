About CHANGE

CHANGE is a non-political, non-profit research center advancing climate health by addressing infectious diseases, antimicrobial resistance (AMR), and contaminants of emerging concern (CECs). We combine field science, genomics, and inclusive design to convert evidence into practical tools for adaptation and preparedness. Our work contributes directly to SDG 3 (Good Health & Well-Being), SDG 6 (Clean Water & Sanitation), and SDG 13 (Climate Action).

Our Vision

Communities most exposed to climate shocks can anticipate health threats early, adapt with dignity, and thrive-benefiting from reliable water, fewer infections, and equitable access to care.

Our Mission

To co-create science-based solutions that cut risk from climate-sensitive infections and pollutants. We fuse environmental monitoring, pathogen genomics, and social inclusion to build equitable early-warning, stewardship, and pandemic-preparedness systems.

Strategic Objectives

1) Climate Health & Adaptation

Link climate hazards to infectious-disease risks and guide locally owned adaptation that protects the most exposed.

2) AMR Stewardship

Track resistance, map transmission, and evaluate credible alternatives (e.g., phages) to preserve antibiotic effectiveness.

3) CECs & Clean Environments

Monitor microplastics and chemical residues; co-design practical exposure-reduction and water-safety measures.

4) Early Warning & Dashboards

Develop no/low-tech indicators with communities and pair them with digital dashboards for timely, local action.

5) Pandemic Preparedness

Strengthen detection, data pipelines, and response playbooks that scale from neighborhoods to national systems.

6) Gender Equity & Inclusion

Ensure women, youth, and marginalized groups shape priorities and share equally in benefits and opportunities.

7) Science Valorization

Translate research into policy briefs, protocols, open tools, and investable solutions that deliver real-world impact.

8) Capacity & Partnerships

Train local actors and sustain north–south–south collaborations for ethical, long-term stewardship of data and systems.

Departments

Our work is organized into interdisciplinary departments that integrate research, implementation, and capacity building.

Climate Health

Department of Climate Change & Adaptation

Quantifying climate–health relationships and co-developing locally grounded adaptation strategies to reduce exposure to waterborne and environmentally driven infections.

AMR

Department of Antimicrobial Resistance

Genomics-enabled surveillance, transmission mapping across human–environment interfaces, and development of sustainable antimicrobial stewardship and alternative interventions.

CECs

Department of Contaminants of Emerging Concern

Monitoring microplastics, chemical residues, and environmental contaminants, and assessing their interactions with microbial risks and human health exposure pathways.

Early Warning

Department of Early Warning Systems & Data Analytics

Designing community-based early warning systems integrating environmental signals, pathogen data, and digital tools to support timely, locally actionable decisions.

Preparedness

Department of Pandemic Preparedness & Response

Developing field-ready surveillance workflows, genomic detection pipelines, and response frameworks to strengthen preparedness across community, health system, and policy levels.

Equity & Impact

Department of Gender Equity & Science Valorization

Embedding gender-responsive approaches and translating research into policy, practice, and scalable solutions to ensure equitable impact and uptake.

Gender Equality & Inclusion

CHANGE is committed to promoting gender equality, inclusive participation, and a safe and respectful working environment across all its activities. This commitment is reflected in our Gender Equality Plan (GEP), which guides our institutional culture, recruitment practices, leadership development, research design, and partnerships.

Public Commitment

Our Gender Equality Plan is formally endorsed by top management and made publicly available through this website.

Dedicated Resources

CHANGE assigns responsibility for gender and inclusion implementation within its leadership and project structures, and will engage relevant expertise where needed.

Monitoring & Reporting

We are committed to collecting and reviewing sex-disaggregated data on participation, roles, and leadership, and to using indicators to monitor progress over time.

Training & Awareness

We promote staff and leadership awareness on gender equality, inclusion, and unconscious bias through internal orientation, training, and capacity-building activities.

Gender in Research

We integrate gender considerations into research and innovation wherever relevant, especially in work on climate health, infectious diseases, antimicrobial resistance, environmental exposure, and community vulnerability.

Safe Working Environment

CHANGE maintains zero tolerance for discrimination, gender-based violence, and sexual harassment, and is committed to respectful and equitable collaboration.
