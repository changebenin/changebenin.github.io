About CHANGE

CHANGE is a non-political, non-profit research center advancing climate health by addressing infectious diseases, antimicrobial resistance (AMR), and contaminants of emerging concern (CECs). We combine field science, genomics, and inclusive design to convert evidence into practical tools for adaptation and preparedness. Our work supports the SDGs—especially SDG 3, SDG 6, and SDG 13.

Our Vision

Communities most exposed to climate shocks can anticipate health threats early, adapt with dignity, and thrive—benefiting from reliable water, fewer infections, and equitable access to care.

Our Mission

To co-create science-based solutions that cut risk from climate-sensitive infections and pollutants. We fuse environmental monitoring, pathogen genomics, and social inclusion to build equitable early-warning, stewardship, and pandemic-preparedness systems.

Strategic Objectives

1) Climate Health & Adaptation

Link climate hazards to infectious-disease risks and guide locally owned adaptation that protects the most exposed.

2) AMR Stewardship

Track resistance, map transmission, and evaluate credible alternatives (e.g., phages) to preserve antibiotic effectiveness.

3) CECs & Clean Environments

Monitor microplastics and chemical residues; co-design practical exposure-reduction and water safety measures.

4) Early Warning & Dashboards

Develop no/low-tech indicators with communities and pair them with digital dashboards for timely, local action.

5) Pandemic Preparedness

Strengthen detection, data pipelines, and response playbooks that scale from neighborhoods to national systems.

6) Gender Equity & Inclusion

Ensure women, youth, and marginalized groups shape priorities and share equally in benefits and opportunities.

7) Science Valorization

Translate research into policy briefs, protocols, open tools, and investable solutions that deliver real-world impact.

8) Capacity & Partnerships

Train local actors and sustain north–south–south collaborations for ethical, long-term stewardship of data and systems.
